## Allophony
The non-palatalised consonants may all also be interpreted as velarised, either alongside the labialisation or in place of it, realising /m/ as [mˠ] for example.

Vowel allophony is entirely up to participants.

## Phonotactics and Prosody
The language has a maximal syllable structure of (C)V(C), where only the vowel V is obligatory. Vowels are never long as roots, but may come into contact at boundaries. Roots are at most disyllabic.

Stress is regularly found on the penultimate syllable, including the initial syllable of a disyllabic case suffix and various parts of the verbal paradigm.
