# Original Passage

After receiving another complaint about the quality of his wares, the copper merchant had been feeling morose. As he sat watching his apprentice working hard, he realised hex felt more than admiration for himy. As the apprentice looked back at himx, hey looked like one of the statues carved into the side of the mountain by the river. Between the shade of the whistling jali and the rustling of a nearby grove of mopane trees, nobody would catch them. Nervously, the merchant took a deep breath, and walking slowly crossed the warehouse floor. The apprentice started to ask forgiveness, but the merchant placed a finger on his lips. "Don't worry, this shipment of copper ingots and cloth will also arrive late," said the merchant, confidently reaching for an embrace, before playfully striking his arse with a thwack.
